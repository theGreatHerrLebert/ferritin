# AMBER96 vs BALL on crambin

Operational case writeup for the AMBER96-vs-BALL CI claim in
`claims/forcefield_amber_ball.yaml`.

## Problem

Proteon ships an AMBER96 force-field implementation
(`proteon-connector/src/forcefield/`). Two questions need separate
oracles:

1. **Are the bonded terms numerically right?** This is the BALL
   claim's job — pin bond, angle, and torsion against an independent
   AMBER96 implementation on a fixed structure (crambin).
2. **Are the totals right at scale?** That is the
   `validation/amber96_oracle.py` job (release-tier, vs OpenMM, on
   1000 PDBs) — covered by a separate claim.

Both are needed because component-level parity on one structure
doesn't guarantee total parity at scale, and total parity at scale
can mask compensating component errors.

## Trust Strategy

Validation. BALL (BiochemicalAlgorithms.jl, hildebrandtlab) is an
independent Julia AMBER96 implementation — different team, different
language, different parameter-file vintage from OpenMM. That makes it
a useful **second** oracle, not the primary one: OpenMM remains
proteon's authoritative AMBER96 reference (per the release-tier
claim). BALL pins the components where BALL itself agrees with OpenMM,
so a regression in proteon would surface against either. Where BALL
is known to diverge from the OpenMM-canonical AMBER96 (electrostatic
~20%, impropers due to single-vs-double wildcard), the tolerance is
deliberately wide and the divergence is documented.

- **Oracle**: BALL Julia. Reference values regenerated by
  `tests/oracle/ball_energy_raw.jl test-pdbs/1crn.pdb`. Last
  regenerated 2026-04-18.
- **Fixture**: crambin (1crn), single-chain, raw PDB (no
  reconstruction, no minimization).
- **Engine settings**: `nonbonded_cutoff=1e6` to match BALL's
  no-cutoff reference, independent of proteon's 15 Å performance
  policy.

## Evidence

`tests/oracle/test_ball_energy.py::TestBallEnergyOracle` runs seven
assertions on a single `proteon.compute_energy(s, ff="amber96",
nonbonded_cutoff=1e6)` call:

- `bond_stretch`, `angle_bend`: <1% relative. Measured ~0.7% / ~0.5%
  on crambin; 1% buys margin without letting a regression slip.
- `torsion`: <2.5% relative. Looser than bonded because proper torsion
  is sensitive to small parameter-file differences (phase angles,
  multiplicities). Measured ~1.6%.
- `vdw`: <2.5% relative. Measured ~1.2% at NoCutoff.
- `electrostatic`: <25% relative. **Wide by design.** BALL diverges
  ~20% from OpenMM-canonical AMBER96 (77998 vs 93874 kJ/mol on
  crambin). Most likely cause is partial-charge dictionary or 1-4
  scaling convention. Proteon matches OpenMM (the canonical
  reference); the 25% band catches a catastrophic charge regression
  without breaking on the known BALL gap.
- `improper_torsion`: one-sided sanity. Proteon enforces AMBER's full
  double-wildcard improper lookup and so picks up ~100 amide-plane
  impropers (`* * N H`) that BALL's single-wildcard implementation
  misses on every peptide bond. Asserted: `0 < proteon_improper <
  20 kJ/mol` and `proteon_improper >= BALL_improper`. Pre-fix proteon
  was ~2.2 kJ/mol (close to BALL); post-2026-04-13 fix it is
  ~8.1 kJ/mol (matches OpenMM canonical).
- `total_is_sum`: |total − Σcomponents| < 0.1 kJ/mol — internal
  accounting, not an oracle assertion. Useful as a guard but does
  not contribute to the claim.
- `all_components_finite`: numerical sanity, also internal.

## Assumptions

- The BALL Julia reference values in `BALL_CRAMBIN_RAW` are produced
  by the BALL commit named in the test file's docstring
  (2026-04-18). A newer BALL release that moves the reference would
  invalidate the cited percentages without proteon changing.
- BALL produces output in kJ/mol (asserted by inspection of
  `ball_energy_raw.jl`); a unit regression there would systematically
  skew every comparison.
- The `nonbonded_cutoff=1e6` settings in proteon and `NoCutoff` in
  BALL produce mathematically identical no-cutoff long-range
  evaluation; no Ewald, no reaction field.
- Crambin's small size (46 residues, ~330 heavy atoms) is sufficient
  to exercise the parameter-lookup machinery for every standard
  amino acid bond/angle/torsion class plus the amide-plane improper.
  Larger or non-standard structures may surface convention gaps not
  visible here.

## Failure Modes

- **Single fixture.** Crambin only. A regression specific to non-
  standard residues, terminus handling, or large-loop conformations
  would not surface. The OpenMM release claim covers that
  distribution at scale.
- **BALL is not the canonical oracle.** The 25% electrostatic band
  and the one-sided improper assertion are concessions to BALL's own
  divergence from canonical AMBER96. A reviewer mistaking these wide
  tolerances for evidence of "proteon agrees with the AMBER96 spec"
  would be misled. The release-tier OpenMM claim is the
  authoritative source for that.
- **BALL reference drift.** `BALL_CRAMBIN_RAW` values are baked into
  the test file. Re-running `ball_energy_raw.jl` against a newer BALL
  could change every number; the comment requires a manual
  regeneration with the date noted.
- **No corpus_sha.** Crambin lives at `test-pdbs/1crn.pdb` in the
  proteon source tree, so the source SHA pins it transitively. Still
  worth flagging as a tightening target if proteon's source tree
  reorganises.

## Lessons

- Two oracles cover different failure modes. BALL catches
  parameter-table regressions and bonded-term math; OpenMM catches
  total-energy regressions and convention drift at scale. Citing only
  one would leave gaps that are not mutually invisible.
- Document the convention gaps **inside the claim**, not in a wiki.
  The `electrostatic` 25% band and the improper one-sided assertion
  are unusual and need to be readable from the manifest by anyone
  reviewing whether the claim is honest.
