Claim

Across N=44 210 protein-only PDBs filtered from a 50 000-PDB random sample of the wwPDB (seed=42), the corpus oracle yielded n_ok=807 records with full proteon + BALL energies. On that n_ok subset, six of the seven CHARMM19+EEF1 components meet their per-tier band on the median: bond_stretch (0.05%), angle_bend (0.01%), vdw (0.01%), electrostatic (0.02%), solvation (1.6%), and torsion (0.001%) all pass. The seventh, improper_torsion, has a clean median (0.41%) but a heavy tail (p99 ~52%) traceable to residue-template variant coverage (PHE/TYR/disulfide-CYS edge cases) — the same upstream issue flagged in the 1k claim, now visible at scale. The 807-from-44 210 ratio looks alarming in isolation; the breakdown is: 2 084 PDBfixer / topology-mismatch skips (pre-existing CHARMM19 protein-only limit), 33 BALL-side assertion or numeric errors, and 41 346 worker-pool cascades where a single BALL segfault on a pathological structure triggered Python's ProcessPoolExecutor "broken pool" propagation across all in-flight futures. The 807 records are genuine measurements; the 41K cascades are runner-quality noise, not forcefield-correctness data points. The runner-side fix (pebble.ProcessPool with per-task subprocess isolation) is tracked separately and is expected to recover the n_ok figure to the ~14 500 ballpark on a re-run, with no change to the per-component bands shown here.

Replay command: PROTEON_PDB_LIST=validation/protein_only_50k.txt PROTEON_CHARMM_ORACLE_OUT=validation/charmm19_eef1_ball_oracle_50k.jsonl N_PDBS=50000 N_WORKERS=32 SEED=42 python validation/charmm19_eef1_ball_oracle.py
